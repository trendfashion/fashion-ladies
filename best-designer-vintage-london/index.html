Discover The Best Designer Vintage London Has To Offer

Written by Eveline Aug 30, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you a fashion lover looking for unique and high-quality pieces that stand the test of time? Do you appreciate the craftsmanship and attention to detail that goes into every vintage item? If so, then you're in luck because London is home to some of the best designer vintage shops in the world.

For many people, the idea of buying vintage clothing can be a bit intimidating. They may worry about finding the right fit or style, or they may not know where to begin their search. But fear not, because with a little guidance and know-how, you can score some amazing vintage finds that will elevate your wardrobe to the next level.

So, what exactly is designer vintage? It refers to clothing, bags, and accessories that were made by high-end fashion brands and are now considered vintage or pre-owned. These pieces have a unique history and often tell a story, making them all the more special and desirable.

In this article, we'll take a closer look at the world of designer vintage in London, including where to shop and what to look for. We'll also share some personal experiences and tips to help you find the perfect pieces for your wardrobe.

What to Expect When Shopping for Designer Vintage in London

When it comes to shopping for designer vintage in London, you can expect to find a wide range of options, from high-end boutiques to flea markets and everything in between. Some of the most popular areas for vintage shopping include Camden, Notting Hill, and Brick Lane, but there are also plenty of hidden gems scattered throughout the city.

One of the best things about shopping for vintage in London is the variety of styles and eras you can find. Whether you're looking for a 1920s flapper dress or a 1980s power suit, you're sure to find something that catches your eye. And because vintage pieces are often one-of-a-kind, you can be sure that you won't run into anyone else wearing the same thing.

Another perk of shopping for vintage in London is the quality of the pieces you can find. Many high-end brands from the past were known for their attention to detail and use of high-quality materials, making vintage pieces durable and long-lasting. Plus, by buying pre-owned items, you're doing your part to reduce your carbon footprint and promote sustainable fashion.

Where to Shop for Designer Vintage in London

As we mentioned earlier, there are plenty of places to shop for designer vintage in London. Here are a few of our favorites:

1. Rellik

Rellik is a high-end vintage boutique located in Notting Hill. They specialize in designer pieces from the 1950s to the 1990s and have a wide range of options for both men and women. Their prices can be a bit steep, but the quality of the pieces is top-notch.

2. Beyond Retro

Beyond Retro is a vintage chain with locations throughout London. They have a huge selection of vintage clothing, shoes, and accessories from a variety of eras and styles. Their prices are more budget-friendly than some other vintage shops, making it a great option for those looking to dip their toes into the world of vintage.

3. Portobello Road Market

The Portobello Road Market in Notting Hill is a must-visit for vintage lovers. On Saturdays, the market is dedicated to antiques and vintage items, including clothing and accessories. You never know what you'll find here, but the thrill of the hunt is part of the fun.

4. William Vintage

William Vintage is a high-end vintage boutique located in Marylebone. They specialize in designer pieces from the 1930s to the 1990s and have dressed everyone from Rihanna to Amal Clooney. Their prices are definitely on the higher side, but the quality and rarity of their pieces make it worth it for some.

Tips for Shopping for Designer Vintage in London

Now that you know where to shop for designer vintage in London, here are a few tips to help you make the most of your shopping experience:

1. Do Your Research

Before you head out to shop, do some research on the brands and styles you're interested in. This will help you narrow down your search and make it easier to spot the pieces you're looking for.

2. Try Everything On

Since vintage sizing can be inconsistent, it's important to try everything on before you buy. Don't be discouraged if something doesn't fit perfectly - many vintage pieces can be altered to fit you like a glove.

3. Inspect the Item Carefully

When shopping for vintage, it's important to inspect the item carefully for any damage or signs of wear. Make sure to check for stains, tears, and missing buttons, and ask the seller about the item's history if possible.

4. Don't Be Afraid to Haggle

Many vintage sellers are open to negotiation, so don't be afraid to ask for a lower price if you feel it's warranted. Just be polite and respectful in your approach.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it safe to buy vintage clothing?

A: Yes, it's safe to buy vintage clothing as long as you take the proper precautions. Make sure to inspect the item carefully for any damage or signs of wear, and wash or dry clean the item before wearing it.

Q: How do I know if a vintage piece is authentic?

A: Authentic vintage pieces should have labels or tags that indicate the brand and era. You can also do some research on the brand and style to ensure that it's consistent with the time period.

Q: Can vintage clothing be altered?

A: Yes, many vintage pieces can be altered to fit you better. Just make sure to find a skilled tailor who has experience working with vintage clothing.

Q: How do I care for vintage clothing?

A: Vintage clothing should be washed or dry cleaned according to the care label instructions. It's also a good idea to store vintage pieces in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent fading and damage.

Conclusion of Best Designer Vintage London

Shopping for designer vintage in London is a unique and rewarding experience that can lead to some amazing finds. By following our tips and recommendations, you'll be well on your way to building a wardrobe full of high-quality, one-of-a-kind pieces that you'll cherish for years to come.
