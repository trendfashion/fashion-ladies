Haro Bikes

When it comes to old school BMX brands, Haro Bikes is a name that consistently comes up. Founded in 1978 by Bob Haro, the brand quickly gained popularity for its innovative designs and high-quality components. Today, Haro Bikes continues to be a leader in the BMX world with a range of bikes that cater to riders of all ages and skill levels.

Personally, I've always been a fan of Haro Bikes. My first BMX bike was a Haro, and I've been riding their bikes ever since. What sets Haro Bikes apart from other brands is their commitment to quality and their attention to detail. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned pro, you can count on Haro Bikes to deliver a bike that meets your needs.

GT Bicycles

Another top old school BMX brand is GT Bicycles. Founded in 1979 by Gary Turner, GT Bicycles has been a favorite among riders for over 40 years. The brand is known for its innovative designs and high-quality components, making it a popular choice for riders of all skill levels.

My first experience with GT Bicycles was when I was a teenager. I saved up for months to buy a GT BMX bike, and it was worth every penny. The bike was incredibly well-built and lasted for years. What I appreciate about GT Bicycles is their commitment to quality and their willingness to push the boundaries of what's possible in the world of BMX biking.

Redline Bicycles

Founded in 1974 by Linn Kastan, Redline Bicycles has been a fixture in the BMX world for over 40 years. The brand is known for its innovative designs and high-quality components, making it a top choice among riders of all skill levels.

Mongoose Bicycles

Question and Answer

Q: What makes old school BMX bikes different from modern BMX bikes?

A: Old school BMX bikes are typically characterized by their retro designs, whereas modern BMX bikes tend to have a more streamlined, futuristic look. Additionally, old school BMX bikes may not have some of the features that modern BMX bikes have, such as disc brakes or suspension systems.

Q: Are old school BMX bikes still relevant?

A: Absolutely! While modern BMX bikes have their advantages, there's something special about riding an old school BMX bike. These bikes have a certain charm and character that modern bikes can't replicate.

Q: Can you still buy old school BMX bikes?

A: Yes! While some old school BMX bikes may be harder to find than others, there are still plenty of options available. You can often find old school BMX bikes for sale online or at specialty bike shops.

Q: What should I look for when buying an old school BMX bike?

A: When buying an old school BMX bike, it's important to look for a bike that's in good condition and has been well-maintained. You should also consider the brand and the components that the bike has. Finally, make sure that the bike fits your size and riding style.

Conclusion of Best Old School BMX Brands

When it comes to choosing the best old school BMX brand, it ultimately comes down to personal preference. However, by considering the brands mentioned in this guide, you can narrow down your options and find a bike that meets your needs. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned pro, there's an old school BMX bike out there for you. Happy riding!
