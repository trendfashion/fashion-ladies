Best Stores In The 80 S A Trip Down Memory Lane

Remember the days of big hair, neon colors, and leg warmers? The 80's was a decade of vibrant fashion, music, and culture. It was also a time when shopping malls were all the rage, and people flocked to the stores to buy the latest trends. However, with so many options, it could be overwhelming to decide which stores were the best. In this post, we will take a trip down memory lane and explore the best stores in the 80's.

If you are a fan of 80's fashion, you know that it was all about making a statement. From acid-washed jeans to oversized blazers, people wanted to stand out from the crowd. However, finding the perfect outfit was not always easy. With so many stores to choose from, it was easy to feel lost in a sea of options. That is why it is important to know which stores offered the best selection and customer service.

The target of this post is to provide readers with a list of the best stores in the 80's. We will explore each store's unique offerings and what made them stand out from the rest. By the end of this post, readers will have a better understanding of where to go for their next 80's fashion fix.

The Gap

As one of the most popular stores in the 80's, The Gap was known for its classic and timeless fashion. The store offered a wide range of clothing for men, women, and children, making it a one-stop-shop for families. The Gap was also known for its high-quality denim, which was a must-have item during that time. The store's clean and minimalist aesthetic made it stand out from other stores cluttered with trendy items. I remember going to The Gap with my family and feeling like we were buying something that would last a long time.

Benetton

Benetton was a unique store that offered a range of colorful clothing. The store was known for its bright sweaters that came in every color of the rainbow. Benetton was also known for its controversial ad campaigns that tackled social issues such as racism and LGBTQ+ rights. The store's commitment to social justice made it stand out from other stores that were only focused on selling clothes. I remember going to Benetton with my friends and feeling like we were part of a movement.

Contempo Casuals

Contempo Casuals was a store that was geared towards teenage girls. The store offered a range of trendy clothing that was affordable and fashionable. Contempo Casuals was known for its bright colors, bold patterns, and oversized accessories. The store was a favorite among teenage girls who wanted to stay on-trend without breaking the bank. I remember going to Contempo Casuals with my friends and feeling like we were part of a fashion-forward group.

Sam Goody

Sam Goody was a record store that offered a wide range of music. The store was known for its knowledgeable staff who could recommend the latest and greatest albums. Sam Goody was also a place where people could go to discover new music and connect with other music lovers. The store's commitment to music made it stand out from other stores that were only focused on selling merchandise. I remember going to Sam Goody with my brother and feeling like we were part of a community of music lovers.
