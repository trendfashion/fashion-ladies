Best Sustainable Running Brands A Guide To Eco Friendly Fitness

Written by Bardi Aug 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a runner who cares about the environment? Do you want to make sure that your fitness routine is sustainable? Look no further than the best sustainable running brands, which combine high-performance gear with eco-friendly materials and ethical manufacturing practices.

When it comes to sustainable running, there are a few pain points to consider. First, many athletic brands use synthetic materials that are harmful to the environment during production and disposal. Second, the manufacturing process for athletic wear can be exploitative, with workers paid low wages and subjected to poor working conditions. And finally, some sustainable running brands have a reputation for being expensive and difficult to find.

But with the rise of eco-conscious consumers and the growing demand for sustainable products, many brands have stepped up to meet the needs of runners who care about the environment. Whether you're looking for shoes made from recycled materials, clothing that's free from harmful chemicals, or gear that's manufactured in fair trade factories, there are plenty of options available.

In this article, we'll explore the best sustainable running brands and what sets them apart from the rest. We'll cover everything from the materials they use to their manufacturing practices to their price points, giving you a comprehensive guide to eco-friendly fitness.

Patagonia

As one of the pioneers of sustainable fashion, Patagonia has a well-deserved reputation for producing high-quality outdoor gear that's kind to the environment. Their running line is no exception, with products made from recycled polyester, organic cotton, and other sustainable materials.

Personally, I love Patagonia's running shorts, which are made from a blend of recycled polyester and spandex. They're lightweight, breathable, and have a secure pocket for storing keys or energy gels. Plus, Patagonia is committed to fair labor practices, ensuring that their workers are paid a fair wage and treated with respect.

Brooks

Brooks has been making running shoes for over a century, and they've recently made a commitment to sustainability. Their shoes are made with recycled materials, and they've started using a biodegradable foam in their midsoles to reduce their environmental impact.

I recently tried out Brooks' Ghost 13 running shoes, which are made with 30% recycled materials. They're comfortable, supportive, and have great traction on both road and trail surfaces. Plus, Brooks has a strong commitment to social responsibility, partnering with organizations like Girls on the Run to promote fitness and confidence in young girls.

Veja

If you're looking for sustainable running shoes that are also stylish, look no further than Veja. This French brand uses organic cotton, wild rubber, and recycled polyester to create shoes that are both eco-friendly and fashion-forward.

I recently purchased a pair of Veja's Venturi sneakers, which are made with organic cotton and wild rubber harvested from the Amazon rainforest. They're comfortable, lightweight, and have a unique design that stands out from other running shoes. Plus, Veja is committed to transparency in their manufacturing process, ensuring that workers are paid fair wages and treated ethically.

Girlfriend Collective

For sustainable running apparel that's both comfortable and affordable, check out Girlfriend Collective. This brand uses recycled materials to create leggings, sports bras, and other activewear that's both eco-friendly and stylish.

I own a pair of their high-rise leggings, which are made from recycled water bottles and have a compressive fit that's great for running. They're also available in a wide range of sizes, making them a great choice for runners of all shapes and sizes. Plus, Girlfriend Collective is committed to ethical manufacturing practices, ensuring that their workers are paid a living wage and have safe working conditions.

Question and Answer

Q: Are sustainable running brands more expensive than traditional athletic brands?

A: It depends on the brand and the specific product, but in general, sustainable running brands can be more expensive than traditional athletic brands. However, many sustainable brands offer high-quality products that are designed to last longer, which can ultimately save you money in the long run.

Q: How can I find sustainable running brands that fit my budget?

A: Look for brands that offer sales or discounts, and consider purchasing from secondhand or consignment stores. You can also try renting running gear instead of buying it outright.

Q: What should I do with my old running gear?

A: If your gear is still in good condition, consider donating it to a local charity or thrift store. If it's no longer wearable, look for recycling programs that accept athletic wear, such as those offered by Patagonia and Nike.

Q: How can I encourage more sustainable practices in the running community?

A: Share your knowledge and experiences with others, and advocate for more sustainable practices in your local running groups and events. You can also support sustainable brands and encourage others to do the same.

Conclusion of Best Sustainable Running Brands

When it comes to sustainable running brands, there are plenty of options available for runners who care about the environment. From Patagonia's eco-friendly outdoor gear to Veja's stylish running shoes, there's something for everyone. By choosing sustainable brands, we can reduce our impact on the environment and support ethical manufacturing practices. So why not make your next run a sustainable one?
