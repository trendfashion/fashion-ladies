Famous Biggest Designers Of The 90S Trend In 2023

Written by Daniel Nov 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Remember the days when the fashion world was dominated by the biggest designers of the 90s? From grunge to glamour, these designers set the trends for the decade and left a lasting impact on the industry. In this blog post, we're going to take a trip down memory lane and explore some of the most iconic designers of the 90s.

Why are the biggest designers of the 90s still relevant today?

While the 90s may seem like a distant memory, the influence of these designers can still be felt in the fashion world today. Many of the trends that they popularized, such as oversized denim jackets, slip dresses, and crop tops, have made a comeback in recent years. Additionally, their innovative use of fabrics, prints, and textures has inspired a new generation of designers to push the boundaries of fashion.

Tom Ford: The King of Glamour

When it comes to the biggest designers of the 90s, Tom Ford is a name that immediately comes to mind. As the creative director of Gucci and later Yves Saint Laurent, Ford revolutionized the fashion industry with his daring designs and provocative ad campaigns. His signature style was all about glamour, with slinky silk dresses, velvet blazers, and sky-high stilettos dominating the runway. Even today, Ford remains a major force in the industry, with his eponymous brand encompassing everything from clothing to fragrance to eyewear.

Marc Jacobs: The Master of Grunge

If Ford was the king of glamour, then Marc Jacobs was the king of grunge. As the creative director of Perry Ellis in the early 90s, Jacobs caused a stir when he sent a collection of flannel shirts and ripped denim down the runway. The collection was widely panned by critics, but it struck a chord with a generation of disaffected youth. Jacobs went on to launch his own label, which became known for its playful, irreverent designs and collaborations with artists like Stephen Sprouse and Takashi Murakami.

Alexander McQueen: The Enfant Terrible

With his theatrical runway shows and avant-garde designs, Alexander McQueen was one of the most controversial and influential designers of the 90s. McQueen's designs were often dark and macabre, with skull motifs, razor-sharp tailoring, and heavily embellished corsets dominating his collections. Despite his untimely death in 2010, McQueen's legacy lives on through his eponymous label, which continues to push the boundaries of fashion with its bold, avant-garde designs.

Donatella Versace: The Queen of Versace

Following the death of her brother Gianni Versace in 1997, Donatella Versace took over as the creative director of the family's eponymous label. Under her direction, Versace continued to be one of the most iconic and influential brands of the 90s, with its bold prints, bodycon silhouettes, and celebrity endorsements. Today, Versace remains a major force in the fashion industry, with Donatella at the helm and a new generation of stars, such as Kendall Jenner and Gigi Hadid, helping to keep the brand relevant.

A: Tom Ford was known for his glamorous, sexed-up designs, which included slinky silk dresses, velvet blazers, and sky-high stilettos.

A: When Marc Jacobs was the creative director of Perry Ellis in the early 90s, he sent a collection of grunge-inspired flannel shirts and ripped denim down the runway, which was widely panned by critics but became a hit with young people.

Q: What was Alexander McQueen's signature style?

A: Alexander McQueen was known for his theatrical runway shows and avant-garde designs, which often featured skull motifs, razor-sharp tailoring, and heavily embellished corsets.

Q: Who took over as the creative director of Versace following Gianni Versace's death?

A: Donatella Versace took over as the creative director of Versace following her brother's death in 1997.

Conclusion of biggest designers of the 90s

The biggest designers of the 90s left an indelible mark on the fashion industry, and their influence can still be felt today. From Tom Ford's glamour to Marc Jacobs' grunge to Alexander McQueen's avant-garde designs, these designers pushed the boundaries of fashion and inspired a new generation of creatives. Whether you lived through the 90s or are discovering these designers for the first time, there's no denying the impact that they had on the industry and the lasting legacy that they leave behind.
