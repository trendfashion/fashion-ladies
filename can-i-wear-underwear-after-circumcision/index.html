Can I Wear Underwear After Circumcision? The Answer You Need to Know

If you're about to undergo circumcision or have just had the procedure done, you're likely wondering about the do's and don'ts of post-surgical care. One of the most common questions people have is whether they can wear underwear after circumcision. It's a valid concern, as the last thing you want is to cause further discomfort or hinder the healing process. In this post, we'll answer your burning question and address related concerns.

The pain and discomfort that come with circumcision can range from mild to severe depending on various factors, such as the technique used, age, and individual pain tolerance. In some cases, the pain can last for several days, and this can make it challenging to perform even the simplest of tasks. Wearing underwear can be uncomfortable, especially if it's tight or made from rough material. It can also cause friction and rubbing, which can irritate the wound area and slow down the healing process.

The good news is, you can wear underwear after circumcision, but it's critical to choose the right type of underwear. Your best option is to wear loose-fitting, breathable underwear that won't cause friction or irritation. Cotton underwear is an excellent choice as it's soft, comfortable, and breathable. You should avoid wearing tight-fitting or synthetic underwear, as these can trap moisture and bacteria, leading to infection and irritation.

When Can You Start Wearing Underwear After Circumcision?

It's best to avoid wearing underwear for the first few days after circumcision to allow the wound area to heal and reduce the risk of infection. After a few days, you can start wearing loose-fitting, breathable underwear. You should continue to avoid tight-fitting or synthetic underwear until the wound has fully healed.
