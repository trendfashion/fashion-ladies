Classic Easy Tiramisu Recipe: A Step-by-Step Guide

As a personal experience, I always loved tiramisu but thought it was too complicated to make at home. However, after trying this recipe, I realized how simple it can be! The ingredients are easy to find, and the process is straightforward.

To make a classic easy tiramisu recipe, you'll need the following ingredients:

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pound mascarpone cheese
  • 1/2 cup strong brewed coffee or espresso
  • 24-30 ladyfingers
  • Cocoa powder, for dusting

Steps:

Step 1: Make the custard

In a medium saucepan, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until well combined. Add the milk and whisk until all ingredients are combined. Cook the mixture over medium heat, whisking constantly, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon, about 10 minutes. Remove the pan from heat and let it cool for 5 minutes.

Tip:

Be sure to whisk the mixture frequently to avoid any lumps. Also, don't let the mixture boil, as this can cause it to curdle.

Step 2: Whip the cream

In a large mixing bowl, beat the heavy cream and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form. Add the mascarpone cheese and beat until well combined.

Tip:

Be careful not to overbeat the cream, as this can cause it to become grainy.

Step 3: Combine the custard and cream

Gently fold the cooled custard into the whipped cream mixture until well combined.

Tip:

When folding the custard into the cream, be gentle to avoid deflating the mixture.

Step 4: Assemble the tiramisu

Dip each ladyfinger into the coffee or espresso and place it in a single layer in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Spread half of the cream mixture over the ladyfingers. Repeat with another layer of soaked ladyfingers and the remaining cream mixture. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Tip:

Be sure to dip the ladyfingers quickly, as they can become too soggy if left in the coffee or espresso for too long.

Step 5: Serve

When ready to serve, dust the top of the tiramisu with cocoa powder.

Tip:

To make the tiramisu look even more impressive, you can pipe additional whipped cream on top.

History of Tiramisu

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert that has been enjoyed for decades. It is believed to have originated in the Veneto region of Italy in the 1960s. The word "tiramisu" means "pick me up" or "lift me up" in Italian, which is fitting as the dessert is often served after a heavy meal to help digestion.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I use a substitute for mascarpone cheese?

A: Yes, you can use cream cheese or ricotta cheese as a substitute for mascarpone cheese. However, the flavor and texture of the tiramisu may be slightly different.

Q: Can I use decaf coffee or espresso?

A: Yes, you can use decaf coffee or espresso if you prefer.

Q: How long will tiramisu last in the fridge?

A: Tiramisu will last in the fridge for up to 3 days.

Q: Can I freeze tiramisu?

A: No, tiramisu does not freeze well and can become soggy.
