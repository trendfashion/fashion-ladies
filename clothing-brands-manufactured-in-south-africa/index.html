Clothing Brands Manufactured In South Africa A Guide

Written by William Jul 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you want to support South African businesses and buy locally-made clothing? Are you tired of the same old international brands dominating the market? Look no further! In this guide, we will introduce you to some of the best clothing brands manufactured in South Africa.

Many people are concerned about the impact of globalisation on local industries, and the fashion industry is no exception. Cheap labour and mass production in other countries have led to a decline in local clothing manufacturing, with many South Africans finding it difficult to access quality, locally-made clothing.

The target of this guide is to introduce you to the best South African clothing brands that are made locally, ethically, and sustainably.

In summary, this guide is a comprehensive resource for anyone interested in supporting South African businesses and buying locally-made clothing. We will explore the best South African clothing brands, their production methods, and their commitment to sustainability and ethical practices.

1. Good Clothing

Good Clothing is a Cape Town-based brand that produces sustainable, ethical, and locally-made clothing for both men and women. Their clothing is made using natural fabrics such as organic cotton, hemp, and linen, and they use non-toxic dyes to minimise their environmental impact. I recently bought a linen shirt from Good Clothing, and not only did I feel good wearing it, but I also felt good knowing that it was made in South Africa using sustainable practices.

2. The Joinery

The Joinery is a Johannesburg-based brand that produces high-quality, locally-made clothing for men and women. They use sustainable fabrics such as bamboo, hemp, and organic cotton, and they are committed to ethical production practices. I have a pair of bamboo leggings from The Joinery, and they are so comfortable and long-lasting.

3. Lunar

Lunar is another Cape Town-based brand that produces locally-made clothing using sustainable and ethical practices. Their garments are made using natural fabrics such as linen, and they use natural dyes to minimise their environmental impact. Lunar is committed to transparency in their production process, and they even have a "Trace Your Garment" feature on their website that allows you to see exactly where your garment came from.

4. Esse

Esse is a Durban-based brand that produces locally-made clothing using sustainable and ethical practices. They use natural fabrics such as linen and organic cotton, and they are committed to transparency in their production process. Esse also has a zero-waste policy, which means that they use every scrap of fabric, and they even offer a recycling program for their clothing.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Are these brands affordable?
A: While these brands may be slightly more expensive than fast fashion brands, they are affordable for the quality and sustainability of their clothing. Plus, by buying locally-made clothing, you are supporting South African businesses and contributing to the local economy.

Q: Can I find these brands online?
A: Yes, all of these brands have online stores where you can purchase their clothing.

Q: Are these brands only available in South Africa?
A: While these brands are all based in South Africa, they may ship internationally. Check their websites for more information.

Q: Why is it important to buy locally-made clothing?
A: Buying locally-made clothing supports local businesses and contributes to the local economy. It also reduces the carbon footprint of clothing production and supports sustainable and ethical practices.

Conclusion of Clothing Brands Manufactured in South Africa

Supporting South African businesses and buying locally-made clothing is important for the economy and the environment. By choosing to purchase clothing from brands that are committed to sustainability and ethical practices, you are making a positive impact on the world. So why not give these South African clothing brands a try and see for yourself how good it feels to wear clothing that is made with care and attention to detail?
