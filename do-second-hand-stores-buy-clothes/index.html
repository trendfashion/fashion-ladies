Do Second Hand Stores Buy Clothes A Guide To Selling Your Clothes

Written by Daniel Jul 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you in need of some extra cash or looking to declutter your closet? Selling your clothes to second hand stores can be a great solution. However, the process can be confusing and overwhelming, leaving you wondering, "Do second hand stores even buy clothes?"

The answer is yes, many second hand stores do buy clothes. These stores purchase gently used clothing items from individuals and resell them in their stores. It's a win-win situation; you get to make some money and clear out your closet, and the store gets inventory to sell.

So, if you're looking to sell your clothes, what do you need to know? Here are some tips and tricks for selling your clothes to second hand stores.

How to Prepare Your Clothes for Selling

Before you head to the second hand store, it's essential to prepare your clothes for selling. This will increase the likelihood of the store buying your items and the amount they offer you. Here are some things to keep in mind:

1. Clean your clothes: Make sure your clothes are freshly laundered and free of stains and odors. Clothes that are dirty or have a strong smell will likely be rejected by the store.

2. Repair any damages: If your clothes have any damages, such as holes or missing buttons, take the time to repair them. Clothes in good condition will fetch a higher price.

3. Sort your clothes: Separate your clothes into piles of similar items, such as dresses, pants, and shirts. This will make it easier for the store to assess your items.

How to Find Second Hand Stores That Buy Clothes

Now that you've prepared your clothes, it's time to find a second hand store that buys clothes. Here are some ways to locate stores:

1. Google search: Do a quick search for second hand stores in your area, and check their websites to see if they buy clothes.

2. Yelp: Look up second hand stores on Yelp and filter your search to only show stores that buy clothes.

3. Social media: Check out local buy/sell/trade groups on Facebook or Instagram. Many second hand stores advertise their buying services on these platforms.

What to Expect When Selling Your Clothes

When you arrive at the second hand store, a buyer will assess your items and make you an offer. Here are some things to keep in mind:

1. Don't expect to get rich: Second hand stores typically pay a fraction of what you paid for your clothes. Don't expect to make a fortune, but do expect to make some money.

2. Be prepared to negotiate: If the store offers you a price that you're not happy with, don't be afraid to negotiate. Just be reasonable and don't expect to get an offer much higher than the original.

3. Don't take it personally: If the store doesn't want to buy your items, don't take it personally. It doesn't mean your clothes aren't great, it just means they don't fit the store's current inventory needs.

Question and Answer

Q: What types of clothes do second hand stores typically buy?

A: Second hand stores typically buy gently used, on-trend clothing items. This includes items such as dresses, pants, shirts, and jackets.

Q: Do second hand stores buy designer clothing?

A: Yes, many second hand stores do buy designer clothing. However, the price they offer will likely be less than the original retail price.

Q: Can I sell my clothes online instead of going to a second hand store?

A: Yes, there are many online marketplaces, such as Poshmark and ThredUp, where you can sell your clothes. However, keep in mind that these platforms typically take a commission from your sales.

Q: What should I do with clothes that don't sell?

A: If your clothes don't sell at the second hand store, consider donating them to a local charity or thrift store.

Conclusion

Selling your clothes to second hand stores can be a great way to make some extra cash and clear out your closet. By preparing your clothes, finding the right store, and knowing what to expect, you can have a successful selling experience. Happy selling!
