Discovering The Beauty Of Eastern Areas Of Pakistan

Written by Christine Oct 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered what lies beyond the bustling cities of Pakistan? If you're looking for a peaceful and serene escape, then the eastern areas of Pakistan might just be the perfect destination for you. From stunning landscapes to rich cultural heritage, this region has it all.

The eastern areas of Pakistan are often overlooked by travelers due to the lack of awareness about the region. Additionally, many people believe that the area is unsafe due to its proximity to the border with India. However, this couldn't be further from the truth. The region is safe to travel to and offers a unique cultural experience that cannot be found anywhere else in the country.

If you're looking to explore the eastern areas of Pakistan, there are a few things that you should keep in mind. Firstly, it's important to respect the local culture and traditions. Additionally, it's a good idea to travel with a local guide who can show you around and help you navigate the area.

In this article, we'll be taking a closer look at the eastern areas of Pakistan and what makes them such a special destination for travelers.

Discovering the Beauty of Hunza Valley

Located in the northernmost part of Pakistan, Hunza Valley is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the eastern areas of the country. The valley is known for its stunning natural beauty, with snow-capped mountains and crystal clear lakes at every turn.

During my visit to Hunza Valley, I was struck by the warmth and hospitality of the locals. From sharing a cup of tea with a local family to hiking to the top of the mountains, the experience was nothing short of magical.

Exploring the Ancient City of Taxila

Situated just outside the capital city of Islamabad, Taxila is an ancient city that dates back to the 6th century BC. The city was once a major center of learning and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

During my visit to Taxila, I was amazed by the intricate carvings and architecture of the ancient buildings. The city is a true testament to the rich cultural heritage of the eastern areas of Pakistan.

Understanding the Local Culture

One of the most unique aspects of the eastern areas of Pakistan is the local culture. From the cuisine to the clothing, the region has a distinct identity that is unlike any other part of the country.

During my travels, I had the opportunity to attend a local wedding and was blown away by the vibrant colors and traditions. It was a truly unforgettable experience that gave me a deeper appreciation for the local culture.

Getting Around the Eastern Areas of Pakistan

While the eastern areas of Pakistan are relatively safe, it's important to be careful when traveling around the region. The best way to get around is by hiring a local guide who can show you the best places to visit and keep you safe.

Additionally, it's important to respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites. This will help you avoid any unwanted attention and show respect for the local culture.

Conclusion of Eastern Areas of Pakistan

The eastern areas of Pakistan are a hidden gem that are just waiting to be discovered. From the stunning natural beauty of Hunza Valley to the rich cultural heritage of Taxila, the region has something to offer every traveler.

So why not plan a trip to the eastern areas of Pakistan and experience the beauty and hospitality of this unique part of the country for yourself?

Question and Answer

Q: Is it safe to travel to the eastern areas of Pakistan?

A: Yes, the eastern areas of Pakistan are relatively safe to travel to. However, it's important to be careful and respectful of the local culture.

Q: What is the best time to visit the eastern areas of Pakistan?

A: The best time to visit the eastern areas of Pakistan is from April to October, when the weather is mild and the natural landscape is at its best.

Q: What should I wear when visiting the eastern areas of Pakistan?

A: It's best to dress modestly when visiting the eastern areas of Pakistan, especially when visiting religious sites. Women should wear long sleeves and pants or a long skirt, while men should wear long pants and a shirt.

Q: Is it necessary to hire a local guide when visiting the eastern areas of Pakistan?

A: While it's not necessary to hire a local guide, it's highly recommended. A local guide can show you the best places to visit and help you navigate the area safely.
