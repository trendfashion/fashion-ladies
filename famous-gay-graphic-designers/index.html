David Hockney

David Hockney is a British artist and designer who is widely regarded as one of the most influential artists of the 20th century. He is openly gay and has used his art to explore his sexuality and challenge societal norms. Hockney is best known for his paintings of swimming pools and landscapes, but he has also worked extensively in graphic design, creating posters, book covers, and album art.

One of Hockney's most iconic designs is the cover of the 1967 album "Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band" by The Beatles. The cover features a colorful collage of famous figures, including Hockney himself. The design has become a cultural icon and is widely regarded as one of the greatest album covers of all time.

Paul Rand

Paul Rand is an American graphic designer who is best known for his work on logos and corporate identities. He is credited with creating some of the most recognizable logos in the world, including those for IBM, ABC, and UPS. Rand was openly gay and faced discrimination throughout his career, but he remained dedicated to his work and is widely regarded as one of the most influential designers of the 20th century.

One of Rand's most famous designs is the logo for IBM, which he created in 1956. The logo features the letters "IBM" in a bold, sans-serif font, and has become a symbol of modernity and innovation. The design has remained largely unchanged for more than 60 years and is still in use today.

Keith Haring

Keith Haring was an American artist and designer who was known for his bold, colorful designs and his activism on behalf of the LGBTQ+ community. Haring was openly gay and used his art to raise awareness of social issues, including HIV/AIDS and gay rights. He was also a prolific designer, creating posters, murals, and album art.

One of Haring's most iconic designs is his "Radiant Baby" image, which features a crawling baby with lines radiating from its body. The image has become synonymous with Haring's work and has been reproduced on everything from t-shirts to skateboard decks.
