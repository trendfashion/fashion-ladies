How To Make Your First Street Brand Contact A Beginner s Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 06, 2023 · 5 min read

As a beginner in the world of streetwear, making your first street brand contact can be intimidating. You may be unsure of how to approach a brand, what to say, or even what to expect. This is a common feeling, but it doesn't have to hold you back from making valuable connections in the industry.

One of the biggest concerns when it comes to making your first street brand contact is not knowing where to start. You may be overwhelmed by the number of brands out there and unsure of which ones to reach out to. Additionally, you may be nervous about making a bad impression or not knowing what to say.

The target of your first street brand contact should be to introduce yourself and your passion for streetwear. This can be done through email or social media, depending on the brand's preferred method of contact. Your message should be brief but thoughtful, introducing yourself and expressing your admiration for the brand. It's important to show that you have done your research and are familiar with the brand's values and aesthetic.

In summary, making your first street brand contact can be nerve-wracking, but it's an important step in building your network within the industry. The key is to be thoughtful and genuine in your approach, and to show that you are passionate about streetwear.

Why Make Your First Street Brand Contact?

As a streetwear enthusiast, making your first brand contact is an essential step towards building your network and establishing yourself within the industry. By reaching out to brands, you can learn more about the industry and potentially even secure collaborations or job opportunities in the future.

Personally, my first street brand contact was with a small, independent brand that I had been following on social media for a while. I reached out to them through Instagram direct message, introducing myself and expressing my admiration for their work. To my surprise, they responded quickly and we ended up chatting about the industry and their design process. While this interaction didn't lead to anything concrete, it was a valuable learning experience and helped me feel more confident in reaching out to other brands in the future.

How to Make Your First Street Brand Contact

The first step in making your first street brand contact is to research the brands you are interested in. Look for brands that align with your personal style and values, and that you genuinely admire. Once you have a list of brands you would like to contact, research their preferred method of contact - this could be through email, Instagram direct message, or even their website's contact form.

When crafting your message, be sure to introduce yourself and express your admiration for the brand. Show that you have done your research by mentioning specific products or collections that you particularly enjoyed. It's also a good idea to briefly explain why you are reaching out - for example, if you are interested in collaborating or working with the brand in the future.

What to Expect When Making Your First Street Brand Contact

When making your first street brand contact, it's important to keep your expectations realistic. While it's possible that your message will lead to a collaboration or job opportunity, it's more likely that you will simply receive a polite response thanking you for your interest. Don't be discouraged by this - building relationships within the industry takes time and effort.

Tips for Making a Successful First Street Brand Contact

Here are a few tips to help you make a successful first street brand contact:

  • Do your research - make sure you are familiar with the brand's values, aesthetic, and products
  • Be genuine - express your admiration for the brand in a sincere way
  • Keep it brief - brands receive a lot of messages, so make sure your message is to the point
  • Don't be discouraged by a lack of response - building relationships takes time and effort

Question and Answer

Q: Should I reach out to multiple brands at once?

A: It's generally a good idea to start with one or two brands at a time, to make sure you are able to give each message the attention it deserves. Once you become more comfortable with the process, you can start reaching out to more brands.

Q: What should I do if I don't receive a response?

A: Don't be discouraged - brands receive a lot of messages, so it's possible that your message simply got lost in the shuffle. You can try following up after a week or so, but if you still don't receive a response, it's best to move on and focus on other brands.

Q: Should I mention that I am looking for job opportunities?

A: It's generally not necessary to mention job opportunities in your first message to a brand. Instead, focus on introducing yourself and expressing your admiration for the brand. If the brand is interested in working with you in the future, they will likely ask for more information about your skills and experience.

Q: What should I do if a brand asks me to collaborate?

A: If a brand expresses interest in collaborating with you, be sure to carefully consider the opportunity before accepting. Make sure that the collaboration aligns with your personal values and goals, and that you are able to meet any deadlines or requirements.

Conclusion of First Street Brand Contact

Making your first street brand contact can be intimidating, but it's an important step towards building your network within the industry. By doing your research, being genuine in your approach, and keeping your expectations realistic, you can make valuable connections and potentially even secure collaborations or job opportunities in the future.
