Paul Rand

Paul Rand is one of the most influential graphic designers of all time. He is best known for his iconic logos, including those for IBM, ABC, and UPS. Rand was a pioneer of the modernist design movement and believed that "good design is good for business." He was known for his minimalist approach to design, using simple shapes, bold colors, and typography to create memorable logos.

My personal experience with Paul Rand's work has been through his iconic IBM logo, which is still in use today. I appreciate his approach to design, and his emphasis on simplicity and function.

Milton Glaser

Milton Glaser is another influential graphic designer who has made a significant impact on the industry. He is best known for his "I Love NY" logo, which has become an iconic symbol of New York City. Glaser was a co-founder of the influential Push Pin Studios and has worked on a range of projects, from book covers to restaurant branding.

My personal experience with Milton Glaser's work has been through his "I Love NY" logo, which I have seen countless times in my travels to New York City. I appreciate his ability to create a simple yet powerful design that has become a cultural icon.

David Carson

David Carson is a graphic designer who has made a significant impact on the industry by challenging traditional design practices. He is best known for his work on Ray Gun magazine, which featured experimental typography and layout designs. Carson's work was a departure from the clean, minimalist designs of the modernist movement and embraced a more chaotic, expressive style.
