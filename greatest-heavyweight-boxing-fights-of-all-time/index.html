The Thrilla in Manila: Ali vs. Frazier III

The Thrilla in Manila was the third and final fight between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. The fight took place on October 1, 1975, in Manila, Philippines. The bout was one of the most brutal fights in boxing history, and it was a true test of the fighters' wills and determination. Ali won the fight by TKO in the 14th round, but both fighters were battered and bruised by the end of the bout.

The Rumble in the Jungle: Ali vs. Foreman

The Rumble in the Jungle was a fight between Muhammad Ali and George Foreman. The fight took place on October 30, 1974, in Kinshasa, Zaire (now the Democratic Republic of the Congo). The fight was one of the most significant events in sports history and marked the peak of Ali's career. Ali won the fight by knockout in the eighth round, and the bout is considered one of the greatest upsets in boxing history.

Mike Tyson vs. Buster Douglas

Mike Tyson was one of the most feared heavyweight boxers of all time, with a record of 37 wins and no losses. On February 11, 1990, he faced off against James "Buster" Douglas in Tokyo, Japan. Douglas was considered an underdog, but he shocked the world by knocking out Tyson in the 10th round. The fight is considered one of the greatest upsets in sports history and marked the end of Tyson's dominance in the sport.

Lennox Lewis vs. Vitali Klitschko

The fight between Lennox Lewis and Vitali Klitschko took place on June 21, 2003, in Los Angeles, California. Lewis was the reigning heavyweight champion, and Klitschko was considered a dangerous opponent. The fight was stopped in the sixth round due to a cut above Klitschko's eye, and Lewis was declared the winner by TKO. The fight is considered one of the most controversial in boxing history, and many believe that Klitschko was winning the fight before the stoppage.

The Conclusion of Greatest Heavyweight Boxing Fights of All Time

The greatest heavyweight boxing fights of all time have left an indelible mark on the sport and the world.
