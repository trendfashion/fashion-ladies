How Much Do Product Designers Get Paid A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Oct 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a product designer? Or maybe you're already a product designer and you're wondering if you're being paid fairly for your work. Whatever the case may be, the question of how much do product designers get paid is a common one. In this article, we'll dive into the topic of how much product designers earn and what factors affect their salaries.

Product designers often face challenges related to their pay, such as companies who don't value their work or a lack of transparency around salary negotiations. But the biggest challenge can be figuring out what a fair salary is for their skillset and experience.

How Much Do Product Designers Get Paid?

The answer to this question varies depending on a few different factors. According to Glassdoor, the average base pay for a product designer in the United States is around $90,000 per year. However, this varies based on factors such as location, experience, and company size. Product designers in larger cities such as San Francisco or New York City can expect higher salaries, while those in smaller cities or towns may earn less. Experience also plays a role, with senior product designers earning more than entry-level designers. Additionally, the size and type of the company can affect pay, with larger companies typically offering higher salaries than smaller ones.

In summary, product designers can expect to make around $90,000 per year on average, but this number can fluctuate based on various factors.

My Personal Experience

As a product designer with five years of experience, I've found that my salary has increased steadily over the years. When I first started out in the industry, I earned around $60,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved to a larger city, my salary increased to around $110,000 per year. I've also found that negotiating my salary during the hiring process has been crucial in ensuring that I'm being paid fairly for my skillset.

Factors That Affect Product Designer Salaries

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect how much product designers get paid. Here are a few of the most significant factors:

Location

Product designers in larger cities can expect higher salaries due to the higher cost of living in those areas. However, it's important to note that some companies located in smaller cities or towns may offer higher salaries to attract top talent.

Experience

Experience plays a significant role in determining a product designer's salary. Entry-level designers can expect to earn less than senior designers with several years of experience under their belts.

Company Size

The size and type of company can also affect product designer salaries. Larger companies typically offer higher salaries than smaller ones, and product designers who work for startups may receive equity or other compensation in addition to their salary.

Industry

The industry that a product designer works in can also affect their salary. For example, product designers who work in the tech industry may earn more than those who work in the retail industry.

Conclusion of How Much Do Product Designers Get Paid

Overall, product designers can expect to earn around $90,000 per year on average, although this number can vary based on factors such as location, experience, and company size. It's essential for product designers to research average salaries in their area and negotiate their pay when starting a new job to ensure that they're being paid fairly for their skills and experience.

Question and Answer

Q: Do product designers receive bonuses or other forms of compensation in addition to their salary?

A: Yes, some companies offer bonuses or equity as part of a product designer's compensation package.

Q: How can product designers negotiate their salaries?

A: Research average salaries in their area and come prepared with examples of their work and how it has contributed to the company. Be confident in their skills and experience and be willing to walk away from a job offer if the salary isn't fair.

Q: Can freelance product designers expect to earn the same amount as full-time designers?

A: Freelance designers may earn more or less than full-time designers depending on their level of experience and the demand for their services.

Q: How can product designers ensure that they're being paid fairly for their work?

A: Research average salaries in their area and compare their skills and experience to those of other product designers. Negotiate their pay when starting a new job and be willing to walk away from a job offer if the salary isn't fair.
