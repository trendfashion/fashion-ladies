How To Describe Wedding Dress Style In Relaxed English Language

Written by Robby Oct 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Wedding dress shopping can be overwhelming, especially when it comes to describing the style you're looking for. You may have a vision in mind, but struggle with finding the right words to explain it to the bridal consultant. This can lead to frustration and confusion, making the process more difficult than it needs to be.

When it comes to describing wedding dress style, it's important to remember that you don't need to use technical terms or fashion jargon. Instead, focus on using relaxed and descriptive language that accurately conveys the style you're after.

Here are some tips on how to describe wedding dress style in a way that is easy to understand:

Ballgown:

When I was shopping for my wedding dress, I knew I wanted a ballgown-style dress. I described it to the consultant as a dress with a fitted bodice and a full skirt that flares out at the waist. I also mentioned that I wanted the skirt to be made of tulle or organza to give it a princess-like feel. The consultant immediately understood what I meant and was able to pull several dresses that fit the description.

Mermaid:

Another popular style is the mermaid dress. To describe this style, you can say that it fits tightly to the body from the chest to the knee, and then flares out at the bottom. This style is perfect for brides who want to show off their curves.

Sheath:

The sheath dress is a simple and elegant style that is perfect for a beach or outdoor wedding. To describe this style, you can say that it is a long, straight dress that hangs loosely from the shoulders to the hemline. It is usually made of lightweight fabrics like chiffon or silk.

A-Line:

The A-line dress is a classic and versatile style that is flattering on many body types. To describe this style, you can say that it is fitted at the top and flares out gradually from the waist to the hemline, creating an "A" shape. This style is perfect for brides who want a dress that is both elegant and comfortable.

Question and Answer:

Q: How do I describe the neckline of a dress?

A: When describing the neckline of a dress, you can use terms like "sweetheart," "v-neck," "scoop," or "halter."

Q: How do I describe the fabric of a dress?

A: When describing the fabric of a dress, you can use terms like "lace," "satin," "chiffon," or "organza."

Q: How do I describe the length of a dress?

A: When describing the length of a dress, you can use terms like "floor-length," "tea-length," "knee-length," or "mini."

Q: How do I describe the overall style of a dress?

A: When describing the overall style of a dress, you can use terms like "bohemian," "romantic," "modern," or "vintage."

Conclusion:

When it comes to describing wedding dress style, the key is to use relaxed and descriptive language that accurately conveys the style you're after. By using terms like "ballgown," "mermaid," "sheath," and "A-line," you can easily communicate your vision to the bridal consultant. Don't be afraid to ask questions or bring in pictures for reference. With a little bit of guidance, you'll be able to find the perfect dress for your special day.
