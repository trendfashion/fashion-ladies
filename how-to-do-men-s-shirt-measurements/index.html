How To Do Men s Shirt Measurements A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Aug 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of buying shirts that don't fit you well? Do you struggle to find the perfect fit when shopping for men's shirts? The key to finding a well-fitted shirt is knowing your measurements. In this article, we will guide you on how to do men's shirt measurements, so you can confidently purchase the right size for you.

It can be frustrating to buy men's shirts online or in-store and not know if they will fit you well. Ill-fitting shirts can make you feel uncomfortable and unconfident. This is why knowing your measurements is crucial when shopping for shirts. If you're unsure of how to do men's shirt measurements, keep reading.

What You Need:

Before starting the measurement process, make sure you have the following:

  • A measuring tape
  • A friend to assist you (optional)
  • A well-fitted shirt (optional)

How to Measure:

Now that you have your measuring tape and optional items, follow these steps to measure your shirt size:

  1. Collar: Measure around the base of your neck where your collar would sit. Make sure to leave enough room for your finger to fit in between the tape and your neck.
  2. Chest: Measure around the fullest part of your chest, under your armpits and across your shoulder blades.
  3. Waist: Measure around your natural waistline, which is typically located above your belly button and below your ribcage.
  4. Sleeve: Measure from the center of your back, across your shoulder, and down to your wrist. Make sure to keep your arm relaxed at your side when taking this measurement.
  5. Length: Measure from the base of your neck to the bottom hem of your shirt.

Why is It Important to Measure?

Measuring your shirt size is important for two reasons:

  1. It ensures a comfortable fit: A well-fitted shirt will make you feel comfortable and confident, whether you're at work or a social event.
  2. It saves you time and money: Knowing your measurements will prevent you from buying clothes that don't fit well, saving you time and money in the long run.

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

When measuring your shirt size, there are a few common mistakes to avoid:

  • Measuring over clothing: Make sure to measure your bare skin or a thin layer of clothing for accurate measurements.
  • Not keeping the tape straight: Keep the measuring tape straight and snug, but not too tight or too loose.
  • Forgetting to measure your sleeve length: This is a crucial measurement for long-sleeved shirts.

Question and Answer:

Q: Can I measure myself, or do I need someone to assist me?

A: You can measure yourself, but having a friend assist you can make the process easier and more accurate.

Q: Should I measure my shirt size when standing or sitting?

A: Measure your shirt size when standing in a natural posture. Avoid slouching or standing too stiffly.

Q: How often should I measure my shirt size?

A: It's a good idea to measure your shirt size every six months to a year, as your body shape can change over time.

Q: Do I need to measure differently for different types of shirts (e.g., dress shirts, t-shirts)?

A: The measuring process is generally the same for all types of shirts. However, some shirts may have unique features that require additional measurements (e.g., sleeve width for dress shirts).

Conclusion:

Knowing how to do men's shirt measurements is essential for finding a well-fitted shirt that makes you feel confident. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can easily measure your shirt size and avoid the frustration of ill-fitting shirts. Make sure to measure yourself every six months to a year to ensure that your measurements are up-to-date.
