Indian Outfit Names A Beginner s Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

India is a land of diverse cultures and traditions, and one of the most fascinating aspects of this diversity is the variety of traditional Indian outfits. From sarees to salwar kameez, each outfit has a unique name and significance. However, for someone new to Indian culture, the names of these outfits can be overwhelming and confusing. In this blog post, we will break down the most popular Indian outfit names and their meanings in a simple and easy-to-understand manner.

The Target of Indian Outfit Names

As mentioned earlier, the target of this blog post is anyone who is interested in learning about traditional Indian outfits. Whether you are planning to attend an Indian wedding, trying to impress your Indian friends, or simply curious about Indian culture, this guide will provide you with a basic understanding of Indian outfit names and their significance.

Sarees

One of the most iconic Indian outfits is the saree. A saree is a long piece of fabric that is draped around the body in various styles. The most common styles are the Nivi style, which is draped over the left shoulder, and the Bengali style, which is draped over the right shoulder. Some popular types of sarees include:

Banarasi Saree

The Banarasi saree is a popular saree from the city of Varanasi in Uttar Pradesh. It is made of silk and is known for its intricate designs and gold and silver brocade work.

How to Wear a Saree

Wearing a saree can be intimidating for someone who has never done it before. However, with a little practice, it can be quite easy. Here are the steps to drape a saree:

  1. Tuck one end of the saree into the waistband of your petticoat, making sure it is snug.
  2. Wrap the saree around your waist, making sure it is level and the border is at the bottom.
  3. Take the loose end of the saree and make pleats, tucking them into the waistband at the center of the waist.
  4. Bring the remaining saree around your back and over your left shoulder, making sure it is not too tight or too loose.
  5. Secure the saree with a safety pin at the shoulder and adjust it to your liking.

Salwar Kameez

The salwar kameez is a comfortable and versatile Indian outfit that consists of a long tunic (kameez) and loose pants (salwar). Some popular types of salwar kameez include:

Anarkali Salwar Kameez

The Anarkali salwar kameez is a full-length tunic that flares out at the bottom, giving it a flowy and elegant look. It is named after the famous dancer Anarkali, who lived during the Mughal era.

How to Style a Salwar Kameez

One of the best things about the salwar kameez is that it can be styled in many different ways. Here are some tips:

  • Pair a simple kameez with a statement dupatta (scarf) to add some color and texture to your outfit.
  • Wear a fitted kameez with a palazzo-style salwar for a modern and chic look.
  • Accessorize your outfit with some traditional Indian jewelry, such as bangles or jhumkas (earrings).

Kurtis

A kurti is a shorter version of the kameez and is typically worn with leggings or jeans. It is a popular choice for casual and semi-formal occasions. Some popular types of kurtis include:

Angrakha Kurti

The Angrakha kurti is a style that originated in Rajasthan and features a wrap-around design with ties on the side. It is known for its asymmetrical hemline and intricate embroidery.

How to Choose the Right Kurti

When choosing a kurti, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Consider the occasion. For a formal event, opt for a kurti with embellishments or embroidery. For a casual outing, a simple cotton kurti will do.
  • Think about your body type. If you are petite, go for a shorter kurti. If you are tall, a longer kurti will look great on you.
  • Choose the right leggings or pants to go with your kurti. For a dressier look, opt for churidar leggings. For a more casual look, go for straight-leg or palazzo pants.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between a saree and a lehenga?

A: A saree is a long piece of fabric that is draped around the body, while a lehenga is a two-piece outfit that consists of a skirt and a blouse. While both outfits are traditional Indian attire, they are worn for different occasions and have different styles.

Q: What is a choli?

A: A choli is a short blouse that is worn with a saree or a lehenga. It is typically sleeveless and has a low neckline.

Q: What is a dhoti?

A: A dhoti is a traditional Indian garment that is worn by men. It is a long piece of fabric that is wrapped around the waist and legs.

Q: What is a sherwani?

A: A sherwani is a long coat that is worn by men for formal occasions such as weddings and parties. It is typically made of silk or brocade and is worn with a churidar or dhoti.

Conclusion of Indian Outfit Names

We hope that this beginner's guide to Indian outfit names has been helpful in providing you with a basic understanding of traditional Indian attire. While there are many more types of Indian outfits out there, these are some of the most popular ones. Whether you are attending an Indian wedding, cultural event, or simply want to incorporate some Indian fashion into your wardrobe, we encourage you to explore the world of Indian fashion and discover the beauty and diversity of Indian culture.
