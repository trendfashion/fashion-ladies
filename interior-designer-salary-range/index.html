Interior Designer Salary Range How Much Can You Make

Written by Eveline Sep 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career in interior design? Do you want to know what kind of salary you can expect? Interior design is a popular career choice, but many people are unsure about the salary range. In this post, we will explore the interior designer salary range and what factors can affect it.

One of the biggest pain points for people considering a career in interior design is uncertainty about the salary range. It can be difficult to know what to expect in terms of compensation, which can make it challenging to plan for the future.

The interior designer salary range can vary depending on several factors, such as experience, location, and type of employment. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for interior designers in the United States is $56,040 as of May 2020. However, the salary range can go as low as $31,970 and as high as $96,470.

In summary, the interior designer salary range can vary widely depending on several factors such as experience, location, and type of employment. The median annual salary for interior designers is $56,040, but it can range from $31,970 to $96,470.

Factors That Affect Interior Designer Salary Range

Experience is one of the most significant factors that affect interior designer salary range. Generally, the more experience you have, the higher your salary will be. Location is another important factor that can affect salary. Interior designers who work in cities with a higher cost of living tend to earn more than those who work in smaller, less expensive cities. Finally, the type of employment can also impact salary. Interior designers who work for large firms or design agencies tend to earn more than those who are self-employed or work for smaller firms.

When I first started working as an interior designer, I had little experience and was working for a small design firm. As a result, my salary was on the lower end of the range. However, as I gained more experience and started working for larger firms, my salary increased significantly.

Education and Certification

Education and certification can also impact interior designer salary range. Interior designers who have a bachelor's degree in interior design or a related field tend to earn more than those who do not have a degree. Additionally, certification from a professional organization such as the National Council for Interior Design Qualification (NCIDQ) can also increase salary potential.

Personally, I found that having a degree in interior design and becoming certified through NCIDQ helped me to earn a higher salary. Not only did it give me more credibility as a designer, but it also opened up more job opportunities with higher salaries.

Negotiating Your Salary

When it comes to negotiating your salary as an interior designer, there are a few things to keep in mind. Firstly, do your research and know the salary range for your location and experience level. Secondly, be confident and assertive in your negotiations, but also be willing to compromise. Finally, consider negotiating other benefits such as flexible working hours, additional vacation time, or a signing bonus.

Continuing Education and Professional Development

Continuing education and professional development can also impact your salary as an interior designer. By staying up-to-date with the latest design trends and technologies, you can make yourself more valuable to employers and clients. Additionally, attending industry events and networking with other designers can help you to build your reputation and increase your earning potential.

Conclusion of Interior Designer Salary Range

In conclusion, the interior designer salary range can vary widely depending on several factors such as experience, location, and type of employment. The median annual salary for interior designers is $56,040, but it can range from $31,970 to $96,470. By gaining experience, education, and certification, and staying up-to-date with industry trends, you can increase your earning potential as an interior designer.

Question and Answer

Q: Can interior designers make six-figure salaries?

A: Yes, it is possible for interior designers to make six-figure salaries, but it is not common. Interior designers who have many years of experience, work for large firms, or are self-employed and have a large client base can earn six-figure salaries.

Q: How can I negotiate a higher salary as an interior designer?

A: Research the salary range for your location and experience level, be confident and assertive in your negotiations, and consider negotiating other benefits such as flexible working hours, additional vacation time, or a signing bonus.

Q: Is it necessary to have a degree in interior design to become an interior designer?

A: While it is not strictly necessary to have a degree in interior design to become an interior designer, it can help to increase your earning potential and job opportunities. Additionally, certification through a professional organization such as NCIDQ can also boost your credibility and earning potential.

Q: How often should I attend industry events as an interior designer?

A: It is a good idea to attend industry events and network with other designers regularly, such as once or twice a year. This can help you to stay up-to-date with industry trends, build your reputation, and increase your earning potential.
