What is the Education System in Spain?

The education system in Spain is divided into four stages: pre-primary, primary, secondary, and higher education. Higher education is further divided into two stages: undergraduate and graduate studies.

Undergraduate studies are known as Grado and generally last for four years. Graduate studies are known as Máster and usually last for one or two years. Doctoral studies, known as Doctorado, can last for up to three years.

Is College Free in Spain for Spanish Residents?

While college education is not completely free in Spain, Spanish residents are eligible for lower tuition fees than international students. The cost of education varies depending on the institution and the course of study, but on average, Spanish residents can expect to pay around 1,000-1,500 euros per year for undergraduate studies.

How Can International Students Reduce the Cost of Education in Spain?

International students can reduce the cost of education in Spain by applying for scholarships, grants, and financial aid.
