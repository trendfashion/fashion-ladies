Business Casual

Business casual is a style of dress that is less formal than traditional business attire, but still professional. It's typically worn in the office or for business meetings. Business casual attire for men includes dress pants or khakis, a button-down shirt or polo shirt, and dress shoes or loafers. Avoid wearing jeans, t-shirts, and sneakers.

Personally, I had a business meeting with a client and wore dress pants, a button-down shirt, and dress shoes. I felt confident and professional, and the client was impressed with my attire.

Smart Casual

Smart casual is a dress code that is more relaxed than business casual, but still stylish. It's often worn for social events and dinners. Smart casual attire for men includes dress pants or chinos, a collared shirt, and dress shoes or loafers. You can also add a blazer or sport coat for a more formal look.

For a friend's dinner party, I wore chinos, a collared shirt, and dress shoes. I felt comfortable and stylish, and received compliments on my outfit.

Formal

Formal attire is the most dressed-up style of dress, typically worn for black-tie events such as weddings and galas. Formal attire for men includes a tuxedo or a black suit, a dress shirt, and dress shoes. You can also add a bow tie or necktie for a more formal look.

Semi-Formal

Semi-formal attire is a dress code that is less formal than black-tie, but more formal than smart casual. It's often worn for evening events such as cocktail parties and dinners. Semi-formal attire for men includes a dark suit, a dress shirt, and dress shoes. You can also add a tie or bow tie for a more formal look.

Casual

Casual attire is the most relaxed style of dress, typically worn for weekends and social outings. Casual attire for men includes jeans or shorts, a t-shirt or polo shirt, and sneakers or sandals. You can also add a hoodie or sweatshirt for a more casual look.
