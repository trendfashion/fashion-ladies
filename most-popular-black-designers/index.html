Virgil Abloh

Virgil Abloh is a Ghanaian-American designer and entrepreneur who is currently the artistic director of Louis Vuitton's menswear collection. He is also the founder of the streetwear label Off-White. Abloh's designs are known for their minimalist aesthetic and bold graphics. He has collaborated with various brands and artists, including Nike, IKEA, and Kanye West.

Abloh's impact on the fashion industry has been significant, as he has brought a fresh perspective to luxury fashion. He has also been an advocate for diversity and inclusion in the industry, creating opportunities for black designers and models.

Kerby Jean-Raymond

Kerby Jean-Raymond is a Haitian-American designer and founder of Pyer Moss. His designs are known for their political and social commentary, as he often uses his platform to address issues such as police brutality and racism. Jean-Raymond has won various awards for his work, including the CFDA/Vogue Fashion Fund and the Harpers Bazaar ICONS Award.

Through his designs, Jean-Raymond has brought attention to important social issues and has challenged the fashion industry to be more inclusive and diverse. He has also created opportunities for black designers and models, showcasing their work in his shows and campaigns.

Tracy Reese

Tracy Reese is an American designer who has been in the industry for over 30 years. She has dressed various celebrities, including Michelle Obama and Oprah Winfrey. Reese's designs are known for their feminine and colorful aesthetic. She is also a vocal advocate for sustainability and ethical fashion.

Reese has paved the way for future generations of black designers, creating opportunities for them through her mentorship programs and collaborations.
