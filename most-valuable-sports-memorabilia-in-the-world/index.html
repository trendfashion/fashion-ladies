The 1909 Honus Wagner Baseball Card

The Honus Wagner baseball card is one of the most valuable sports memorabilia in the world. Honus Wagner was a baseball player during the early 20th century, and his card was produced by the American Tobacco Company in 1909. However, the card's production was halted, and only a few were made available to the public.

Today, the Honus Wagner baseball card is considered a rare find and is highly sought after by collectors. In 2016, one of these cards was sold for a whopping $3.12 million, making it the most expensive baseball card ever sold.

The 1920 Babe Ruth Jersey

Babe Ruth is one of the most famous baseball players in history, and his 1920 jersey is one of the most valuable sports memorabilia in the world. The jersey was worn by Babe Ruth during his first season with the New York Yankees, and it's the earliest known jersey worn by Ruth. In 2012, the jersey was sold for $4.4 million at an auction, making it the most expensive sports memorabilia ever sold at that time.

The 1970 FIFA World Cup Trophy

The FIFA World Cup Trophy is one of the most iconic sports trophies in the world. However, the original trophy was retired after Brazil won the tournament for the third time in 1970. A new trophy was created for the 1974 World Cup, but the original trophy was never seen again.

In 1983, the original trophy was found in a storage room in Brazil, and it was restored to its original form. Today, the 1970 FIFA World Cup Trophy is one of the most valuable sports memorabilia in the world and is estimated to be worth between $150,000 and $200,000.

The 1936 Jesse Owens Gold Medal

Jesse Owens was one of the most prominent athletes during the 1936 Olympics held in Berlin, Germany. He won four gold medals, and one of them will forever be remembered as a significant moment in history. Owens won the gold medal in the 100 meters race, and he did it in front of Nazi leader Adolf Hitler.

The medal is one of the most valuable sports memorabilia in the world and was sold for $1.47 million in 2013. The sale broke the previous record for the highest price paid for Olympic memorabilia.
