Popular Sunglasses Brands Of The 1980S A Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a fan of the iconic fashion of the 1980s? If so, then you already know that sunglasses were an essential part of any outfit during that time. From oversized frames to neon colors, the sunglasses of the 80s were bold and unique. In this guide, we will take a look at some of the most popular sunglasses brands of the 1980s and what made them so special.

Why Popular Sunglasses Brands of the 1980s Matter

When it comes to fashion, the 1980s were a decade like no other. The clothing, hairstyles, and accessories of that era were all about making a statement and standing out from the crowd. Sunglasses were no exception. The sunglasses of the 1980s were not just a functional accessory to protect your eyes from the sun, but they were also a way to express your individuality and personal style.

Ray-Ban

Ray-Ban is one of the most well-known sunglasses brands in the world, and they were especially popular during the 1980s. The oversized Aviator and Wayfarer frames were favorites of both men and women, and they were often seen on celebrities like Tom Cruise and Madonna. Ray-Ban sunglasses were known for their high-quality lenses and durable frames, and they continue to be popular today.

Oakley

Oakley sunglasses were also popular during the 1980s, particularly among athletes and outdoor enthusiasts. The brand's innovative designs and high-tech materials made them a favorite of skiers, surfers, and cyclists. Oakley sunglasses were known for their polarized lenses, which reduced glare and provided superior clarity, and their lightweight frames.

Revo

Revo was another popular sunglasses brand of the 1980s, known for its polarized lenses and colorful frames. The brand was founded in 1985 and quickly became a favorite of celebrities and outdoor enthusiasts. Revo sunglasses were often seen on the faces of athletes like Dan Marino and Arnold Palmer, and they were known for their high-quality lenses that provided excellent protection from the sun.

Carrera

Carrera sunglasses were a favorite of the fashion-forward crowd in the 1980s. The brand was known for its bold, oversized frames and colorful lenses, which were often seen on the faces of celebrities like Michael Jackson and Elton John. Carrera sunglasses were also popular among athletes, particularly in the world of motor racing.

Personal Experience with Popular Sunglasses Brands of the 1980s

As a child of the 80s, I have fond memories of the bold and colorful sunglasses of that era. My favorite pair of sunglasses was a neon green pair of Oakleys that I wore everywhere, even when it wasn't sunny outside. I loved the way they made me feel like I was part of the cool crowd, and they were always a conversation starter.

When I think back on the popular sunglasses brands of the 1980s, I am reminded of how unique and fun that era was. The sunglasses of that time were a reflection of the bold and adventurous spirit of the 80s, and they continue to inspire fashion trends today.

FAQs about Popular Sunglasses Brands of the 1980s

1. What were the most popular sunglasses brands of the 1980s?

The most popular sunglasses brands of the 1980s were Ray-Ban, Oakley, Revo, and Carrera.

2. What made the sunglasses of the 1980s unique?

The sunglasses of the 1980s were known for their bold and colorful frames, oversized shapes, and polarized lenses.

3. Who wore the popular sunglasses brands of the 1980s?

Celebrities, athletes, and fashion-forward individuals all wore the popular sunglasses brands of the 1980s.

4. Are the popular sunglasses brands of the 1980s still popular today?

Yes, many of the popular sunglasses brands of the 1980s, such as Ray-Ban and Oakley, are still popular today.

Conclusion of Popular Sunglasses Brands of the 1980s

The sunglasses of the 1980s were bold, colorful, and unique, and they continue to inspire fashion trends today. Whether you prefer the classic styles of Ray-Ban or the high-tech materials of Oakley, there is a pair of sunglasses from the 1980s that will suit your personal style. So why not take a trip back in time and embrace the adventurous spirit of the 80s with a pair of iconic sunglasses?
