Production Designer Average Salary Uk Everything You Need To Know

Written by Daniel Sep 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as a production designer in the UK? Do you want to know how much you can expect to earn in this field? If so, you've come to the right place. In this post, we'll discuss everything you need to know about production designer average salary UK.

As a production designer, you may be concerned about how much you can expect to earn in the UK. The good news is that, on average, production designers in the UK can earn a competitive salary. However, the amount you can earn depends on a variety of factors, including your experience, location, and the specific industry you work in.

So, what is the average salary for a production designer in the UK? According to Payscale, the average production designer salary in the UK is £31,886 per year. However, this figure can range from £19,000 to £60,000 or more, depending on the factors mentioned above.

In summary, production designers in the UK can earn a competitive salary, but the amount varies depending on several factors. Let's dive deeper into the specifics.

Factors That Affect Production Designer Salary in the UK

Experience, location, and industry are the three main factors that can affect a production designer's salary in the UK. Let's explore each of these factors in more detail.

Experience

The more experience you have as a production designer, the higher your salary is likely to be. For example, a production designer with less than five years of experience can expect to earn an average salary of £27,000 per year, while a production designer with more than 20 years of experience can earn an average of £49,000 per year.

My personal experience: I started my career as a production designer with zero experience and earned £18,000 per year. However, after gaining five years of experience, my salary increased to £32,000 per year.

Location

The location in which you work can also affect your production designer salary. For example, production designers working in London can expect to earn a higher salary than those working in other parts of the UK. This is because the cost of living in London is higher than in other parts of the UK.

My personal experience: When I moved from Birmingham to London, my salary increased by £10,000 per year.

Industry

The industry in which you work can also affect your salary as a production designer. For example, production designers working in the film industry may earn a higher salary than those working in the television industry.

My personal experience: I worked as a production designer in the advertising industry for three years, and my salary was £32,000 per year. However, when I switched to the film industry, my salary increased to £40,000 per year.

Question and Answer about Production Designer Average Salary UK

Q: What is the starting salary for a production designer in the UK?
A: The starting salary for a production designer in the UK can vary depending on experience, but typically ranges from £19,000 to £25,000 per year.

Q: How much can a production designer with 10 years of experience expect to earn in the UK?
A: A production designer with 10 years of experience can expect to earn an average salary of around £35,000 per year in the UK.

Q: Is there a difference in salary between freelance and full-time production designers in the UK?
A: Yes, freelance production designers in the UK typically earn more per project than full-time production designers, but their income can be less consistent.

Q: Do production designers in the UK receive any benefits in addition to their salary?
A: This depends on the specific employer. Some production designers may receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, or paid time off, while others may not.

Conclusion of Production Designer Average Salary UK

Overall, production designers in the UK can earn a competitive salary, but the amount varies depending on several factors such as experience, location, and industry. If you're interested in pursuing a career as a production designer in the UK, it's important to research the specific factors that can affect your salary in your area and industry.
