Tailored Fit Vs Classic Fit Shirt Which One Is Right For You

Written by Luffy Sep 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Choosing the right shirt can be a difficult task, especially when it comes to deciding between a tailored fit and a classic fit. Both options have their own unique benefits and drawbacks, but which one is the best fit for you? In this blog post, we'll explore the differences between tailored fit vs classic fit shirts and help you make an informed decision.

When it comes to choosing a shirt, there are a few factors to consider. One of the biggest pain points is finding a shirt that fits well and looks good on your body type. This can be especially challenging for those who are in between sizes or have a unique body shape. Another pain point is finding a shirt that is comfortable to wear, without sacrificing style.

The target of this blog post is to help you understand the differences between tailored fit vs classic fit shirts, so you can make an informed decision about which one is right for you.

In summary, tailored fit shirts are designed to fit closer to the body, with a slimmer cut and tapered waist. They are ideal for those who have a slim or athletic build and want a more modern, fitted look. Classic fit shirts, on the other hand, are designed to have a looser fit, with more room in the chest and waist. They are ideal for those who prefer a more traditional, relaxed fit.

Tailored Fit Shirts

When it comes to tailored fit shirts, I have a personal experience to share. I have a slim build, and I always struggled to find shirts that fit me properly. Either the chest was too tight, or the sleeves were too short. That's when I discovered tailored fit shirts. They are designed to fit closer to the body, with a slimmer cut and tapered waist. This means that the shirt fits me perfectly, without any excess fabric bunching up around my midsection.

If you have a slim or athletic build, a tailored fit shirt is a great option. They are also a good choice if you prefer a more modern, fitted look. However, if you have a larger build or prefer a more relaxed fit, a tailored fit shirt may not be the best choice for you.

Classic Fit Shirts

On the other hand, if you prefer a more traditional, relaxed fit, a classic fit shirt may be the best choice for you. I also have a personal experience to share about classic fit shirts. When I was younger, I used to wear classic fit shirts all the time. They were comfortable and had plenty of room in the chest and waist. However, as I got older and my body shape changed, I found that classic fit shirts didn't fit me as well as they used to. They were too loose in some areas and too tight in others.

If you have a larger build or prefer a more relaxed fit, a classic fit shirt is a good option. They are also a good choice if you prefer a more traditional, timeless look. However, if you have a slim build or prefer a more modern, fitted look, a classic fit shirt may not be the best choice for you.

Tailored Fit vs Classic Fit: Which One Should You Choose?

When it comes down to it, the choice between tailored fit vs classic fit shirts comes down to personal preference and body type. If you have a slim or athletic build and prefer a more modern, fitted look, a tailored fit shirt is a great option. If you have a larger build or prefer a more relaxed fit, a classic fit shirt is a good choice.

How to Choose the Right Size

Regardless of whether you choose a tailored fit or classic fit shirt, it's important to choose the right size. A shirt that is too small will be uncomfortable and restrict your movement, while a shirt that is too large will look sloppy and unflattering. To find the right size, measure your chest and waist and consult the brand's size chart. If you're in between sizes, it's usually best to go up a size and get the shirt tailored if necessary.

Conclusion of Tailored Fit vs Classic Fit Shirt

Choosing between a tailored fit vs classic fit shirt can be difficult, but it's important to find the right fit for your body type and personal style. Whether you prefer a more modern, fitted look or a more traditional, relaxed fit, there is a shirt out there that will suit your needs. By understanding the differences between tailored fit vs classic fit shirts and considering your own preferences and body type, you can make an informed decision and find the perfect shirt for you.

Question and Answer

Q: What body type is best suited for a tailored fit shirt?

A: Tailored fit shirts are best suited for those with a slim or athletic build.

Q: What body type is best suited for a classic fit shirt?

A: Classic fit shirts are best suited for those with a larger build or those who prefer a more relaxed fit.

Q: Are tailored fit shirts more expensive than classic fit shirts?

A: It depends on the brand and the quality of the shirt. Some brands may charge more for tailored fit shirts, while others may charge the same for both tailored fit and classic fit shirts.

Q: Can you get a tailored fit shirt if you have a larger build?

A: Yes, some brands offer tailored fit shirts in larger sizes to accommodate a wider range of body types.
