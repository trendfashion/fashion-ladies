Top 5 Black Fashion Designers

Written by Bardi Aug 22, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of seeing the same designers in the fashion industry? Do you want to support and celebrate diversity in fashion? Look no further than these top 5 black fashion designers who are making waves in the industry.

Representation matters, and the fashion industry is no exception. Black fashion designers have been historically underrepresented in the industry, despite their immense talent and creativity. By supporting these designers, we can help break down barriers and create a more inclusive and diverse fashion world.

So, who are the top 5 black fashion designers you should know about? Let's dive in.

1. Kerby Jean-Raymond of Pyer Moss

Kerby Jean-Raymond is the founder and creative director of Pyer Moss, a brand that has been making waves in the fashion industry since its debut in 2013. The brand is known for its socially conscious designs and its commitment to representing black culture in a positive light.

As a personal experience, I had the privilege of attending Pyer Moss' 2019 New York Fashion Week show, which was a powerful tribute to black culture and history. The show featured a gospel choir and a runway show that told the story of the black experience in America.

2. Aurora James of Brother Vellies

Aurora James is the founder and creative director of Brother Vellies, a brand that specializes in handcrafted shoes and accessories made by artisans from around the world. The brand has gained a following for its unique designs and commitment to sustainability and ethical production.

As a personal experience, I own a pair of Brother Vellies shoes and can attest to their quality and comfort. It's inspiring to see a designer who values both style and ethics.

3. Christopher John Rogers

Christopher John Rogers is a rising star in the fashion industry, known for his bold and colorful designs that celebrate femininity and individuality. He was the winner of the 2019 CFDA/Vogue Fashion Fund, which recognizes emerging designers who are making an impact in the industry.

4. Carly Cushnie of Cushnie

Carly Cushnie is the founder and CEO of Cushnie, a luxury womenswear brand known for its sleek and sophisticated designs. The brand has been worn by celebrities such as Michelle Obama, Beyoncé, and Jennifer Lopez.

5. Telfar Clemens of Telfar

Telfar Clemens is the founder and creative director of Telfar, a unisex brand known for its minimalist designs and its commitment to accessibility and inclusivity. The brand's signature Shopping Bag has gained a cult following, with fans including Oprah Winfrey and Solange Knowles.

Why is it important to support black fashion designers?

Supporting black fashion designers is important for several reasons. First and foremost, it helps to create a more diverse and inclusive fashion industry. By celebrating and supporting black designers, we can help break down the barriers that have historically excluded them from the industry.

Additionally, supporting black designers helps to promote creativity and innovation in fashion. Black designers bring unique perspectives and experiences to their designs, which can help to push the industry forward and inspire change.

How can we support black fashion designers?

There are several ways to support black fashion designers. One of the easiest ways is to simply purchase their designs. By supporting their brands financially, we can help to ensure their success and visibility in the industry.

Additionally, we can support black fashion designers by following them on social media, attending their shows and events, and sharing their work with others. By spreading the word about these designers, we can help to create a more inclusive and diverse fashion industry.

Conclusion of Top 5 Black Fashion Designers

Representation matters, and the fashion industry is no exception. By supporting top 5 black fashion designers like Kerby Jean-Raymond, Aurora James, Christopher John Rogers, Carly Cushnie, and Telfar Clemens, we can help to create a more inclusive and diverse fashion world. Let's celebrate and support these designers and help to promote creativity and innovation in the industry.

Question and Answer

Q: Why are black fashion designers underrepresented in the industry?

A: Black fashion designers have historically been excluded from the industry due to systemic racism and bias. This has led to a lack of resources and opportunities for black designers, making it harder for them to succeed in the industry.

Q: How can we encourage more diversity in the fashion industry?

A: We can encourage more diversity in the fashion industry by supporting and celebrating designers from diverse backgrounds, advocating for more representation in the industry, and holding brands and institutions accountable for their lack of diversity.

Q: How can we ensure that black fashion designers are given the same opportunities as their white counterparts?

A: We can ensure that black fashion designers are given the same opportunities as their white counterparts by advocating for more diversity in the industry, supporting black designers financially and socially, and holding brands and institutions accountable for their lack of diversity.

Q: What are some other ways we can promote diversity in fashion?

A: Some other ways we can promote diversity in fashion include supporting models from diverse backgrounds, promoting diversity in fashion media, and advocating for more representation at all levels of the industry, from design to production to marketing.
