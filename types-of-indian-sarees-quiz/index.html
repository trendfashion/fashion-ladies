Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between a Banarasi saree and a Kanjivaram saree?
A: Banarasi sarees are made in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, using golden and silver threads, silk, and zari work, whereas Kanjivaram sarees are made in Kanchipuram, Tamil Nadu, using pure silk, and heavy borders and pallus.

Q: What is the significance of a Chanderi saree?
A: Chanderi sarees are known for their sheer texture, light weight, and rich embroidery. They are named after the town of Chanderi in Madhya Pradesh, where they originated. They are often worn for special occasions and weddings.

Q: What is a Patola saree?
A: Patola sarees are handwoven sarees that are made in Patan, Gujarat, using silk yarn and double ikat technique. They are known for their vibrant colors, geometric patterns, and durability. They are considered a symbol of status and wealth.

Q: What is the difference between a Bandhani saree and a Leheriya saree?
A: Bandhani sarees are tie-dyed sarees that are made in Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Uttar Pradesh, using cotton or silk fabric. They have small dots or circles all over the fabric, and are often worn for festive and cultural occasions. Leheriya sarees are also tie-dyed sarees, but they have a wave-like pattern that resembles a lehera or a musical note. They are popular in Rajasthan and are often worn in the monsoon season.
