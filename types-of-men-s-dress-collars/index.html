Types Of Men s Dress Collars A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to dressing up for formal events, men often overlook the importance of choosing the right dress collar. A dress collar is not just a functional aspect of a dress shirt, but it can also make or break your entire outfit. In this post, we'll take a closer look at the different types of men's dress collars and how to choose the right one for your next event.

Choosing the wrong dress collar can result in discomfort, restricted movement and a general sense of unease. Additionally, a poorly chosen dress collar can also make you look out of place and ruin the entire ensemble. It's important to keep in mind that a dress collar should be chosen based on the shape of your face, body type, event type and personal style preferences.

The main purpose of a dress collar is to frame your face and draw attention to your upper body. A well-chosen dress collar can also help you to look taller, slimmer, and more confident. It's important to choose a collar that complements your overall body shape and face structure.

In this post, we'll cover the different types of men's dress collars, their features, and how to choose the right one for your body shape, face structure, and event type. We'll also provide tips on how to style each collar to create a unique and polished look.

The Classic Point Collar

The classic point collar is the most common dress collar found in men's dress shirts. It features pointed collar tips that point downward and can be worn with or without a tie. This collar is suitable for most face shapes and body types, making it a versatile choice for any formal event.

Personally, I love the classic point collar as it's a timeless and elegant choice that can never go wrong. It's also easy to style, and you can pair it with a variety of tie knots, from the classic four-in-hand knot to the more complex Windsor knot.

The Button-Down Collar

The button-down collar features small buttons that fasten the collar tips to the shirt. This collar is a more casual option and is often seen in business-casual settings. It's suitable for men with round or square face shapes as it helps to elongate the face and create a more balanced look.

I personally love the button-down collar for its versatility and laid-back vibe. It's a great option for those who want to add a touch of personality to their outfit without going overboard.

The Spread Collar

The spread collar features collar tips that are farther apart than the classic point collar. It's a great option for men with narrow or angular face shapes as it helps to create a more balanced look. This collar is often seen in formal events and looks great with a tie.

I personally love the spread collar for its modern and sleek look. It's a great option for those who want to add a touch of sophistication to their outfit without going overboard.

The Club Collar

The club collar is a rounded collar that was popular in the early 20th century. It's a great option for men with angular or square face shapes as it helps to soften the face and create a more balanced look. This collar is often seen in vintage-inspired outfits and can be styled with a variety of tie knots.

I personally love the club collar for its unique and vintage-inspired look. It's a great option for those who want to stand out from the crowd and make a statement with their outfit.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a dress collar without a tie?

A: Yes, you can wear a dress collar without a tie. The classic point collar is a great option for a more laid-back look, while the spread collar is perfect for a more formal look.

Q: How do I choose the right dress collar for my face shape?

A: Generally, men with round or square face shapes should opt for a button-down collar, while men with narrow or angular face shapes should choose a spread collar.

Q: Can I wear a bow tie with any dress collar?

A: You can wear a bow tie with any dress collar, but it's important to choose the right knot for your collar. The classic point collar looks great with a variety of bow tie knots, while the spread collar is perfect for a more complex knot.

Q: Can I wear a dress collar with a sweater?

A: Yes, you can wear a dress collar with a sweater. The classic point collar looks great with a V-neck sweater, while the button-down collar is perfect for a crew-neck sweater.

Conclusion of Types of Men's Dress Collars

Choosing the right dress collar can make all the difference in your outfit. It's important to choose a collar that complements your face shape, body type, and personal style preferences. With this guide, you'll be able to choose the perfect dress collar for your next formal event and create a polished and sophisticated look.
