What Are Good Shoes To Wear If You Have Bunions

Written by Frank Aug 19, 2023 · 3 min read

Do you suffer from bunions? It can be tough to find comfortable shoes that don't cause pain and discomfort. If you're looking for relief, choosing the right footwear is crucial.

So, what are good shoes to wear if you have bunions? In short, shoes that provide ample space for the bunion and don't put pressure on the affected area.

When shopping for shoes, keep in mind the following features: wide and deep toe boxes, soft and flexible materials, and adjustable straps or laces.

In this article, we'll dive deeper into what makes a shoe suitable for bunions, and offer some personal experiences and recommendations.

Wide and Deep Toe Boxes

One of the key features to look for in shoes for bunions is a wide and deep toe box. This allows your toes to spread out naturally, reducing pressure on the bunion. A narrow toe box can exacerbate the problem and cause pain and discomfort.

Personally, I've found that shoes with a round or square toe box work best for me. Pointed shoes are a no-go, as they squeeze my toes together and cause pain.

Soft and Flexible Materials

Another important factor to consider is the material of the shoe. Soft and flexible materials like suede or leather can provide more give and accommodate the bunion. Stiff materials like plastic or vinyl can be uncomfortable and cause friction.

I've had success with shoes made from stretchy materials like mesh, as they adapt to the shape of my foot and don't rub against the bunion.

Adjustable Straps or Laces

Shoes with adjustable straps or laces can also be beneficial for those with bunions. This allows you to customize the fit and loosen or tighten as needed.

Personally, I've found that shoes with velcro straps work well for me. They provide a secure fit without putting pressure on the bunion.

Other Considerations

When shopping for shoes, keep in mind the activity you'll be doing. If you're going for a walk or run, look for athletic shoes with the features mentioned above. If you need a dress shoe, opt for a style with a low or medium heel and a roomy toe box.

It's also important to try on shoes later in the day, when your feet are at their largest. This will ensure a proper fit and prevent any rubbing or discomfort.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I still wear heels if I have bunions?

A: It's not recommended, but if you must wear heels, opt for a lower heel and choose a shoe with ample space in the toe box.

Q: Are there any specific shoe brands that are good for bunions?

A: Yes, there are many brands that offer shoes designed for those with bunions, such as Clarks, New Balance, and Vionic.

Q: Can I wear sandals if I have bunions?

A: Yes, but look for sandals with a wide and deep toe box and adjustable straps. Avoid sandals with a high heel or narrow toe box.

Q: Do I need to wear special orthopedic shoes if I have bunions?

A: Not necessarily. While orthopedic shoes can be helpful, there are many regular shoes on the market that offer the features needed for bunion relief.

Conclusion of What are Good Shoes to Wear if You Have Bunions

Choosing the right shoes can make all the difference when it comes to bunion pain and discomfort. Look for shoes with a wide and deep toe box, soft and flexible materials, and adjustable straps or laces. Keep in mind the activity you'll be doing and try on shoes later in the day for the best fit. With the right shoes, you can find relief and enjoy comfortable feet.
