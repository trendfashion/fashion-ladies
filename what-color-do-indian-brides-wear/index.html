What Color Do Indian Brides Wear

Written by Eveline Oct 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Indian weddings are a colorful and vibrant affair, with numerous ceremonies taking place over a few days. One of the most significant events during an Indian wedding is the bride's attire. The attire of the bride is an essential aspect of the wedding, and it is imperative to choose the right color. In this blog post, we will answer the question, "What color do Indian brides wear?"

Choosing the right color for the bridal attire can be a daunting task. There are several factors to consider, such as the bride's skin tone, the time of day the wedding is taking place, the venue, and the cultural significance of the color. This decision can be even more challenging for non-Indian brides who are marrying into an Indian family and are not familiar with Indian wedding customs.

In Indian culture, red is the traditional color of the bridal attire. Red is considered to be a symbol of love and prosperity and is believed to bring good luck to the couple. However, with changing times and cultural influences, brides are now opting for other colors such as gold, pink, green, and blue.

To summarize, Indian brides traditionally wear red, but brides are now opting for other colors such as gold, pink, green, and blue, depending on their preference and cultural significance.

Why is Red the Traditional Color?

As mentioned earlier, red is the traditional color of the bridal attire in Indian culture. Red symbolizes love, prosperity, and fertility. In Hindu mythology, red is also associated with the goddess of power, Durga. The color red is believed to protect the bride from evil spirits and bring good luck to the couple.

My personal experience with the color red is that it is a bold and vibrant color that exudes confidence and elegance. I chose to wear a red lehenga for my wedding, and it was one of the best decisions I made.

What Other Colors Do Indian Brides Wear?

As mentioned earlier, brides are now opting for other colors such as gold, pink, green, and blue. Gold is a popular choice for brides as it symbolizes wealth and prosperity. Pink is another popular color as it represents love, beauty, and romance. Green represents new beginnings and is a popular choice for brides who want a unique and unconventional look. Blue symbolizes trust, loyalty, and stability and is a popular choice for brides who want a calming and soothing color.

Cultural Significance of Other Colors

In addition to the traditional red color, other colors in Indian culture have cultural significance. Gold is associated with wealth and prosperity, and it is a popular color for weddings. Pink is a symbol of love, beauty, and romance and is also the color of the goddess of love, Rati. Green is the color of nature and symbolizes new beginnings, fertility, and prosperity. Blue is associated with Lord Krishna, who is often depicted wearing blue, and is considered to be a calming and soothing color.

How to Choose the Right Color for Your Bridal Attire?

Choosing the right color for your bridal attire can be overwhelming. Here are some tips to help you choose the perfect color:

  • Consider your skin tone
  • Consider the time of day the wedding is taking place
  • Consider the venue
  • Consider the cultural significance of the color

Conclusion of What Color Do Indian Brides Wear

In conclusion, red is the traditional color of the bridal attire in Indian culture, but brides are now opting for other colors such as gold, pink, green, and blue. The choice of color for the bridal attire is an essential aspect of the wedding, and it is imperative to choose a color that is not only beautiful but also has cultural significance.
