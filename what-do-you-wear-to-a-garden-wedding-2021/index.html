What To Wear To A Garden Wedding 2021

Written by Eveline Sep 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you attending a garden wedding this year and unsure what to wear? Garden weddings are beautiful and romantic, but they can also be tricky when it comes to dressing appropriately. You want to look stylish and comfortable, but also take into consideration the outdoor setting and potential weather conditions.

It's understandable to feel overwhelmed when deciding what to wear to a garden wedding 2021. You want to dress appropriately for the occasion while also feeling confident and comfortable.

So, what do you wear to a garden wedding 2021? The answer depends on the dress code, time of day, and the couple's wedding style. But don't worry, we have got you covered with some helpful tips and outfit ideas that will make you the best-dressed guest at the wedding.

My Experience with Garden Weddings

I have attended several garden weddings over the years, and each one was unique in its own way. One wedding had a bohemian vibe with a relaxed dress code, while another was more formal with a black-tie dress code. The key is to read the invitation carefully and take cues from the couple's wedding style.

For me, the most important thing when deciding what to wear to a garden wedding is to choose a comfortable outfit. Garden weddings can last for several hours, and you don't want to be in uncomfortable shoes or a tight dress the whole time.

Dress Code for Garden Weddings

The dress code for garden weddings can vary depending on the couple's preference. Some may opt for a formal dress code, while others may prefer a more relaxed and casual dress code. It is important to read the invitation carefully to determine the dress code.

If the invitation specifies a black-tie dress code, it is best to wear a formal gown or a long dress. If the dress code is semi-formal, you can opt for a cocktail dress or a dressy jumpsuit. For a casual dress code, a sundress or a maxi dress would be appropriate.

Colors and Prints for Garden Weddings

When it comes to colors and prints for garden weddings, it is best to choose light and bright colors that complement the outdoor setting. Pastel shades, floral prints, and light-colored dresses are perfect for garden weddings. Avoid wearing black or dark-colored outfits as they can be too formal and not appropriate for the outdoor setting.

Accessories for Garden Weddings

Accessories can make or break your outfit for a garden wedding. It is best to opt for comfortable shoes, such as wedges or block heels, as they are easier to walk on grass or gravel. You can also add a statement clutch or a pair of earrings to complete your look. Don't forget to bring a light jacket or a shawl in case the temperature drops in the evening.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear jeans to a garden wedding?

A: No, jeans are not appropriate for a garden wedding. It is best to choose a dress or a jumpsuit that is dressy and comfortable.

Q: Can I wear flat shoes to a garden wedding?

A: Yes, you can wear flat shoes to a garden wedding, but it's best to opt for wedges or block heels as they are more comfortable to walk on grass or gravel.

Q: Can I wear a hat to a garden wedding?

A: Yes, you can wear a hat to a garden wedding, but make sure it complements your outfit and doesn't obstruct other guests' views during the ceremony.

Q: Can I wear a black dress to a garden wedding?

A: It is best to avoid wearing black or dark-colored dresses to a garden wedding as they can be too formal and not appropriate for the outdoor setting. Opt for light and bright colors instead.

Conclusion of What to Wear to a Garden Wedding 2021

Attending a garden wedding can be a beautiful and memorable experience, but it can also be challenging when deciding what to wear. Remember to read the invitation carefully, consider the dress code, and take cues from the couple's wedding style. Choose a comfortable outfit that complements the outdoor setting and opt for light and bright colors and prints. Complete your look with comfortable shoes and chic accessories, and you'll be the best-dressed guest at the wedding.
