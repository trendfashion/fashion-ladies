Discovering What Fashion Was Like In The 70 S

Written by Frank Aug 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you ever wonder what fashion was like in the 70's? This era was known for its unique and adventurous styles that people still draw inspiration from today. From bell-bottoms to platform shoes, the 70's was a decade of fashion that left a lasting impact on the industry.

The 70's was a time of change and social upheaval, and fashion was no exception. People were looking to express themselves in new ways, and fashion became a way to do just that. However, with all the options available, it could be quite overwhelming to navigate the fashion scene in the 70's.

If you're curious about what fashion was like in the 70's, keep reading as we explore the various styles and trends that defined this decade.

The Hippie Style

One of the most iconic styles from the 70's was the Hippie style. It was all about embracing nature, peace, and love. The fashion style included long skirts, flowy dresses, peasant blouses, sandals, and headbands. This style was all about comfort, and people would often wear loose-fitting clothes made from natural materials such as cotton and linen.

Personally, my mom used to tell me stories about her love for the Hippie style. She would often wear long skirts and flowy tops, and she always had a flower tucked behind her ear. It was a style that truly embodied the spirit of the 70's.

The Disco Style

The 70's was also known for the Disco style, which was all about glitz and glamour. This style was popularized by the movie "Saturday Night Fever" and was characterized by platform shoes, bell-bottoms, jumpsuits, and sparkly dresses. The colors were bold and bright, and patterns such as stripes and geometric shapes were popular.

My aunt was a huge fan of the Disco style. She would often wear platform shoes and jumpsuits to parties and dance the night away. It was a style that embodied the energy and excitement of the disco scene.

The Rock and Roll Style

Another style that emerged in the 70's was the Rock and Roll style. This style was all about leather jackets, band tees, ripped jeans, and boots. It was a style that was rebellious and edgy, and it represented a break from the mainstream. This style was popularized by bands such as Led Zeppelin and The Rolling Stones.

My dad was a big fan of the Rock and Roll style. He would often wear leather jackets and boots and would attend concerts of his favorite bands. It was a style that embodied the spirit of rebellion and individuality.

How to Incorporate 70's Fashion into Your Wardrobe Today

If you're looking to incorporate 70's fashion into your wardrobe today, there are a few ways to do it. Firstly, you can try wearing bell-bottoms or flared jeans, which are making a comeback in recent years. You can also try wearing flowy dresses or peasant blouses, which are perfect for the summer months. Lastly, you can try adding some platform shoes or boots to your outfit to add some height and edge.

Question and Answer

Q: What were the most popular colors in the 70's?

A: The most popular colors in the 70's were bright and bold colors such as orange, yellow, and green.

Q: What kind of accessories were popular in the 70's?

A: Accessories such as headbands, scarves, and oversized sunglasses were popular in the 70's.

Q: What kind of hairstyles were popular in the 70's?

A: The most popular hairstyles in the 70's were long and flowy, with bangs and layers. Afros were also a popular hairstyle for people with curly hair.

Q: What kind of makeup was popular in the 70's?

A: Makeup in the 70's was all about bold and bright colors. Blue eyeshadow and bright red lipstick were popular choices.

Conclusion of What Fashion Was in the 70's

The 70's was a decade of fashion that left a lasting impact on the industry. From the Hippie style to the Disco style, this decade was all about self-expression and embracing individuality. If you're looking to incorporate 70's fashion into your wardrobe today, try adding some bell-bottoms, flowy dresses, or platform shoes to your outfit. The fashion of the 70's truly embodied the spirit of the decade and still inspires people today.
