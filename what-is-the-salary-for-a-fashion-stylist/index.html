What Is The Salary For A Fashion Stylist

Written by Eveline Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a fashion stylist but wondering about the potential salary? It's important to understand the earning potential in any profession before committing to it. You may be wondering what factors impact a fashion stylist's salary, and what you can expect to earn in this field.

The salary for a fashion stylist can vary depending on several factors such as experience, location, and type of work. The Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that the median annual salary for fashion designers, which includes fashion stylists, is $75,810. However, salaries can range from $32,740 to $146,230, with the highest earners typically working in the motion picture and video industries.

In addition, freelance fashion stylists may charge by the hour or project, which can impact their overall earnings. It's also important to note that salaries can vary greatly depending on the location of the job. Fashion stylists working in major cities such as New York or Los Angeles may earn more than those working in smaller cities or rural areas.

My Personal Experience

As a freelance fashion stylist with five years of experience, I've found that my earnings have steadily increased over time. When I first started, I charged hourly and earned around $20 per hour. As I gained more experience and built up my portfolio, I started charging by project and was able to earn around $2,000 per project. However, this can vary greatly depending on the client and type of work.

Factors that Impact Salary

As mentioned earlier, several factors can impact a fashion stylist's salary. One of the biggest factors is experience. Entry-level fashion stylists may earn less than those with several years of experience under their belt. Another factor is location. Fashion stylists working in major metropolitan areas may earn more than those in smaller cities or rural areas.

Additionally, the type of work can impact salary. Fashion stylists working in the film or television industry may earn more than those working in retail or editorial. Freelance fashion stylists may also have more earning potential than those who work for a specific company.

Education and Training

While a formal education is not required to become a fashion stylist, it can be beneficial in terms of gaining knowledge and skills. Many fashion stylists have a degree in fashion design, merchandising, or a related field. It's also important to have a solid understanding of fashion trends and the ability to create a cohesive look.

How to Increase Your Earnings as a Fashion Stylist

If you're looking to increase your earnings as a fashion stylist, there are several things you can do. First, gain as much experience as possible by taking on a variety of projects and building up your portfolio. Networking is also important in this industry, so attend events and connect with other professionals in the field.

You can also consider specializing in a specific area of fashion styling, such as editorial, celebrity styling, or personal styling. This can help you stand out from competition and potentially earn more money.

My Final Thoughts

Overall, the salary for a fashion stylist can vary greatly depending on several factors. While it may not be the highest paying career in the fashion industry, it can be a fulfilling and rewarding profession for those with a passion for fashion and style.
