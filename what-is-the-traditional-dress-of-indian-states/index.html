Types of Traditional Dress of Indian States

The traditional dress of Indian states varies from one state to another. Here are some examples:

Kerala

Kerala is known for its traditional dress called Mundu, which is worn by men, and Kasavu saree, worn by women. Mundu is a white cotton cloth draped around the waist, while Kasavu saree is a white saree with a golden border.

How to Wear Mundu?

Wear the Mundu around the waist and take one end of it, bring it around the back and tuck it in the front. Then take the other end, bring it around the back, and tuck it in the front. This way, you will have a neat fold in the front.

Punjab

Punjab is known for its traditional dress called Salwar Kameez, which is worn by both men and women. Salwar Kameez consists of a long tunic called Kameez and loose pants called Salwar.

How to Wear Salwar Kameez?

Wear the Salwar around the waist and tie it with a drawstring. Then wear the Kameez, which is a long tunic, over the Salwar. You can also wear a Dupatta, which is a long scarf, over your head or around your neck.

Tamil Nadu

Tamil Nadu is known for its traditional dress called Saree, which is worn by women. Saree is a long piece of cloth draped around the waist and over the shoulder.

How to Wear Saree?

Start by tucking one end of the Saree into the waistband of your petticoat. Then wrap the Saree around your waist once and tuck it in. Take the other end of the Saree and drape it over your shoulder. Then take the remaining fabric and make pleats, tucking them into the waistband.
